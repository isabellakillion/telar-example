title: "What were the stakes of ending affirmative action?"
---

Scholars examining affirmative action during this period similarly challenged narrow definitions of merit. Researchers argued that standardized test scores and grades alone could not fully measure academic potential or merit because educational achievement is deeply shaped by structural inequality. They emphasized that many critics of affirmative action ignored how racism and segregation continued to influence access to quality schools, stable housing, and educational resources. Rather than promote fairness, so-called “color-blind” admissions policies often reinforced existing inequalities while claiming neutrality.
