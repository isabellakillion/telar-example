---
title: "What implications did ending affirmative action have?"
---

These protests, demonstrations, debates, and legal challenges over the end of affirmative action became even more important after Proposition 209 officially took effect in 1998. The impact on minority admissions across the UC system was immediate. Researchers found that underrepresented minority students experienced substantial declines in their chances of admission at selective UC campuses after the affirmative action ban. Black and Latino admissions dropped sharply at universities such as UC Berkeley and UCLA, even among highly qualified applicants. Their findings challenged claims that race-neutral admissions would maintain diversity while simply increasing fairness.
