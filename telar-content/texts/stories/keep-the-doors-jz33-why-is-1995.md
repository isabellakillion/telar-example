---
title: "Why is 1995 important?"
---

In 1995, advocates for affirmative action were galvanized into mass action when the Board of Regents adopted SP-1, a resolution that would ban UCs from considering race in admissions by 1997. Despite vigorous protests, SP-1 was made into a state-wide public ballot initiative, known as Proposition 209, and banned the use of racial preferences in public education, employment, and contracting statewide in 1996. 

This moment in 1995 was known as the National Day of Action. It was a massive protest that included demonstrations around the state to demand a renewed commitment to diversity at UC campuses. This was only one of the many protests, demonstrations, and debates held throughout the mid-90s over the banning of affirmative action at UCSB. The Daily Nexus, among other newspapers, reported on the array of opinions over affirmative action and the regents’ decision, including statements from both advocates and adversaries of the proposed policy to end race-based admissions.
