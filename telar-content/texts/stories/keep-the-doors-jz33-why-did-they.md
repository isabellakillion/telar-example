---
title: "Why did they support SP-1? "
---

Opponents of affirmative action believed the policy disregarded individual achievement and ignored equal treatment under the law. William Yelles, a Nexus staff writer, wrote that the ideas of the policy "run against the mission of the civil rights and feminist movements, which rightfully advocated that the abilities of individuals should be examined without regard to race or gender, that people should be judged only on their merits." Another student in support of ending affirmative action policies urged: "Let's work to keep college a reward for those who have the inner drive to get the job done."
