---
title: "What's affirmative action?"
---

Affirmative action refers to policies designed to increase opportunities for groups historically excluded or discriminated against on the basis of race, gender, or ethnicity. Beginning in the 1960s during the Civil Rights era, affirmative action sought to address systemic inequalities in employment and education by allowing institutions to consider race as one factor among many in admissions or hiring decisions. Supporters argued these policies helped expand minority access to universities and careers that had long been dominated by white Americans, while critics claimed affirmative action gave unfair advantages and undermined merit-based selection.
